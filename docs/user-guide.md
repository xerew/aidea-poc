# AIDEA User Guide

AIDEA is a training platform that helps teachers learn to **use AI**, **prepare
students for AI**, and **teach about AI**. This guide explains what each type of
user can do.

## The three learning pillars

Every course belongs to one of three pillars:

| Pillar | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| **Teach with AI** | Using AI tools to plan, teach and save time. |
| **Teach for AI** | Preparing students for a world shaped by AI. |
| **Teach about AI** | Teaching students what AI is, including its limits and ethics. |

Courses are also tagged with one or more **subjects** (Mathematics, Physics,
History, Languages, …, or *General / All*) and a **level** (Beginner,
Intermediate, Advanced).

## Accounts and roles

There are four roles:

- **Teacher** — the default role. Learns through courses and a personalised pathway.
- **Content creator** — everything a teacher can do, plus authoring courses and viewing analytics.
- **Administrator** — manages users, roles and platform settings.
- **AIDEA partner** — reviews assignment submissions.

New accounts start as **teachers**. A teacher can request content-creator access
from their profile; an administrator approves or declines it.

## Signing in and choosing your language

1. Open the platform and sign in with your username and password (or register a new account).
2. The interface is available in **nine languages**: English, Greek, French,
   Spanish, Italian, Finnish, Swedish, Norwegian and German. Use the language
   switcher in the header to change it at any time — your choice is remembered.

Course content can also be offered in your language when a creator has
translated it; otherwise it falls back to the original language.

## For teachers

### 1. Onboarding

The first time you sign in, a short setup wizard runs (about two minutes):

1. **Your subject** — pick the subject you mainly teach.
2. **Teaching level** — primary, secondary, higher education, vocational or adult education.
3. **Knowledge check** — a few questions that estimate your current AI competency.
4. **Learning goals** — what you want to get out of the platform (select all that apply).

When you finish, AIDEA calculates your **AI competency level** (Beginner,
Intermediate or Advanced) and builds your personalised pathway.

### 2. Home

Your home page shows:

- A **Continue learning** banner for the course you were last working on.
- **Recommended for you** — courses matched to your profile and activity (only shown when recommendations exist).
- The three pillars with your progress in each.

### 3. Finding and enrolling in courses

Go to **Courses** to browse the full catalogue. You can:

- **Search** by title.
- **Filter** by pillar and by level. By default the level filter is set to your
  own competency level, so you see courses that fit you first.
- See each course's **subjects** as tags on its card.

Open a course to read its description, learning outcomes and module list, then
select **Enroll**.

### 4. Taking a course

Inside a course you work through **modules**, each made of **lessons**. Lesson
types include:

- **Text** — rich formatted reading material.
- **Video** and **PDF** — play/read embedded directly in the page.
- **Image** — shown inline.
- **Quiz** — answer questions and get immediate feedback; your answers are saved.
- **Assignment** — write and submit a response for review (see *AIDEA partners* below).

You can take personal **notes** on each lesson. Mark lessons complete as you go;
your course progress updates automatically, and reaching a module marks the
earlier ones complete.

### 5. My Pathway

**My Pathway** is a personalised, ordered list of courses built for you from:

- your **competency level** (so courses match your ability),
- your **preferred pillar(s)**,
- your **learning goals**, and
- your **subject**.

If you change your preferences (see below), your pathway is rebuilt to match.

### 6. My Learning

**My Learning** collects your enrolments in three groups: a *continue learning*
banner, courses *in progress*, and *completed* courses.

### 7. Profile and preferences

On your **Profile** you can manage:

- **Personal information** — name, email, subject, school, country and more.
- **Learning preferences** — preferred learning format, **preferred pillar**,
  weekly goal, and email/progress notifications. Changing your preferred pillar
  re-shapes both your recommendations and your pathway.
- **Language** and **account settings** (including changing your password).
- A **Content Creator access** request (see below).

Your profile header also shows your current **AI competency level and score**.

### 8. Your AI competency

Your competency score (0–6) starts from the onboarding knowledge check and
changes over time:

- Completing courses **raises** it.
- It can **drop** if you repeatedly fail quizzes, move very slowly, or abandon a
  course mid-way for a long time.

When your score crosses into a new band (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced),
your recommendations and pathway adjust.

### 9. Requesting content-creator access

If you want to author courses, open **Profile → Content Creator access**,
describe why, and submit the request. An administrator reviews it; if approved,
your role changes to content creator and the **Authoring** and **Content
Analytics** sections appear.

## For content creators

Content creators have everything a teacher has, plus two extra areas:
**Authoring** and **Content Analytics**.

### Authoring a course

Open **Authoring** and select **New Course** (or **Import course**). For each
course you set:

- **Title**, **description** and **learning outcomes**.
- **Pillar**, **level** and **subjects** (choose all the subjects the course is for, or *General / All*).
- **Source language** — the language the course is written in.

You are set as the course's **author** automatically; administrators can
reassign authorship if needed.

### Modules and lessons

Within a course you add **modules**, and within each module you add **lessons**.
Drag to reorder. For each lesson pick a type:

- **Text** — write formatted content in the built-in **rich-text editor**
  (headings, bold/italic, lists, links).
- **Video** / **PDF** — paste a URL, or upload a PDF/image file directly.
- **Image** — upload or link an image.
- **Quiz** — add questions, each with options; mark the correct answer(s).
- **Assignment** — write the instructions students will respond to.

A live **preview** shows how each lesson will look to learners.

### Publishing and editing

- **Publish** a course to make it visible to teachers.
- A published course can still be edited by **its author** (and unpublished if needed).
- You can **delete** a course you authored.

### Translating a course

Use the **language bar** in the course editor to translate a course into any of
the supported languages. Translations are produced by the platform's translation
service; you can review and edit the translated text per language. Learners then
see the course in their chosen language, with a fallback to the original.

### Importing and exporting (Excel)

- **Download template** gives you a blank Excel workbook with the correct
  structure and drop-downs.
- **Import course** creates a new draft course from a filled-in workbook.
- **Export** downloads any course as a workbook (useful for bulk editing or transfer).

### Content Analytics

Open **Content Analytics** to see engagement:

- **Summary** cards: total enrolments, completion rate, quiz attempts and courses you created.
- A **per-course breakdown** across the published catalogue.
- For **your own courses**, expand **View teachers** to see each enrolled
  teacher's progress, **time spent**, and their **quiz answers** (what they chose
  versus the correct answer).
- **Download Excel** exports a workbook with one sheet per course and one row per
  enrolled teacher.

## For administrators

Administrators have two management surfaces.

### The admin dashboard (in the app)

The in-app **Admin** area has three tabs:

- **Users** — see every user and change their **role** (teacher, content creator,
  AIDEA partner, admin). You cannot change your own role.
- **Requests** — review pending **content-creator access** requests: approve
  (which promotes the user) or decline with a reason.
- **System** — **Refresh recommendations** re-computes personalised
  recommendations for every teacher immediately, instead of waiting for the
  nightly run.

### The Django admin (`/admin/`)

For deeper configuration, open `/admin/`. From here administrators can manage:

- **Users and profiles** — roles, subject, competency and more.
- **Courses, modules and lessons** — including reassigning a course's author.
- **Subjects** — add, rename, reorder or deactivate subjects in the shared taxonomy.
- **Onboarding questions** — edit the competency knowledge-check questions,
  their options and each option's points. Changes take effect for new onboardings.
- **Learning pathways** — the competency-band templates that name each pathway.
- **Recommendation settings** and **learner-activity settings** — tuning weights,
  the competency-decay rules and quiz thresholds.

> Note: onboarding questions edited here are stored as written; the rest of the
> interface stays translated in every language.

## For AIDEA partners (reviewers)

The **AIDEA partner** role reviews **assignment** submissions. When a teacher
submits an assignment, it enters a review queue. Reviewers (course creators,
AIDEA partners and administrators) can open the queue, read each submission, and
approve it — which marks that lesson complete for the teacher — or return it.

## Frequently asked questions

**I don't see Authoring or Content Analytics.**
Those areas are for content creators. Request access from **Profile → Content
Creator access**; an administrator will review it.

**My recommendations look empty.**
Recommendations appear once you have a profile and some activity. Administrators
can also trigger a refresh from **Admin → System**.

**My pathway didn't change when I changed my preferred pillar.**
It should rebuild automatically. Re-open **My Pathway**; the order reflects your
preferred pillar, goals, subject and competency level.

**A course shows in its original language, not mine.**
Course content is translated per course by its creator. Where a translation
isn't available yet, the original language is shown. The interface itself is
always in your chosen language.

**Can I change the onboarding questions?**
Only administrators can, via the Django admin. See *For administrators*.
